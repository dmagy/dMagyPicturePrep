# People

People in dMPP are shared across your whole Picture Library Folder.

That means you create a person once, then reuse that person anywhere they appear in the archive.

## Why People matter

Identifying people helps turn a folder of image files into a meaningful archive.

People information can help with:

- finding pictures of a person
- understanding family history
- grouping related photos
- estimating ages when dates are available
- preparing future slideshows or displays
- making old photos easier to review later

## Add key people first

You do not need to build a complete family tree before using dMPP.

Start with people who appear often in your pictures.

Good first people to add:

- yourself
- close family members
- frequent friends
- people who appear in many photos

You can add more people as you review.

## People are archive-wide

People are stored in the portable archive data for your Picture Library Folder.

This lets the same People list be used across all working folders inside that archive.

For example, if you add `Grandma Jean` once, you can use her in pictures from:

```text
Family Photos / 1970s
Family Photos / Christmas
Family Photos / Reunions
Family Photos / Scanned Albums
```

## Suggested and Manual modes

dMPP has two ways to work with people:

- **Suggested**
- **Manual**

Use the mode that fits the picture.

## Suggested mode

Suggested mode looks for visible faces and shows numbered face slots.

Use Suggested mode when:

- faces are clear
- people are facing the camera
- you want help moving through ordinary snapshots faster
- dMPP has already learned some faces from your archive

Suggested mode may show a suggested person with a confidence percentage.

A suggestion is still a suggestion. Review it before moving on.

## When Suggested mode is new

Early in a new archive, dMPP may not have enough learned face samples to make useful suggestions yet.

That is expected.

As you identify and save people over time, dMPP can learn from confirmed assignments and begin making better suggestions.

## Suggested mode safety

Before saving or moving to another picture, dMPP may require visible detected faces to be assigned or ignored.

This helps prevent unreviewed faces from slipping through accidentally.

For each visible face, choose one of the appropriate actions:

- assign it to a saved person
- enter a one-off label
- ignore it if it should not be tracked

## Manual mode

Manual mode lets you identify people yourself without relying on face detection.

Use Manual mode when:

- the picture is a large group photo
- faces are turned, blurry, small, or partly hidden
- the picture includes pets, statues, or other non-standard subjects
- you want full control
- Suggested mode is not a good fit

Manual mode works well for row-by-row review.

For example:

```text
Front row: Jean, Robert, Alice
Row 2: Mark, Susan, David
Back row: Unknown man, Karen, Tom
```

## Unknown people

Use an unknown person label when you know someone is in the picture but do not know who they are yet.

Examples:

```text
Unknown woman
Unknown child
Unknown man near truck
Unknown cousin?
```

A useful unknown label is better than leaving the person unmarked, especially in group photos.

## One-off people

A one-off label is useful when you want to label a face in this picture without creating a reusable person in Settings.

Use a one-off label for people who are unlikely to appear again or do not need to be tracked across the archive.

## Pets and non-human subjects

If pets or other important subjects appear often in your archive, you may choose to add them in People Settings when appropriate.

Manual mode is often better for these cases because face detection may not find them reliably.

## Age clues

When a person has birth information saved and the picture has a date or era, dMPP can show age clues.

These can help you confirm whether the date or person assignment seems reasonable.

Age clues are helpful, but they are not proof. Use them as context.

## Wrong suggestions

If dMPP suggests the wrong person, choose the correct person instead.

If a very strong suggested match differs from the person you assign, dMPP may show a warning. This can help you notice when a learned face sample may include a bad example.

## Deleted people and learned faces

If a person is deleted from Settings, learned face samples for that person may still exist.

When dMPP detects that a deleted person is still being suggested, it may show a warning and offer to remove the learned samples for that deleted person.

This helps prevent old or accidental learning from continuing to affect suggestions.

## Missing people references

If a picture sidecar refers to a person who no longer exists in Settings, dMPP may show a People warning.

When possible, dMPP preserves useful details in Curator Notes so the clue is not lost when the picture is saved.

## Good habits

Start simple.

Add people as you encounter them. Use unknown labels when needed. Use Manual mode for complex group photos. Use Suggested mode when faces are clear and the app can help.

Review suggestions carefully before moving on.
