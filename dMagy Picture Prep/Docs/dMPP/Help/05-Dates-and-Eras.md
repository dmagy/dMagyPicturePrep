# Dates and Eras

Use Date Taken or Era to record what you know about when a picture was taken.

You do not need an exact date. A useful approximate date is better than leaving the picture undated.

## Why dates matter

Dates help dMPP and future dMagy tools understand the picture in context.

Dates can help with:

- sorting pictures
- searching and filtering
- understanding family timelines
- estimating people’s ages when birth information is available
- identifying pictures that may belong to the same event or time period

## Supported date styles

dMPP supports several practical date formats.

```text
1984-07-04
```

Use this for an exact date.

```text
1984-07
```

Use this when you know the year and month.

```text
1984
```

Use this when you only know the year.

```text
1980s
```

Use this when you only know the decade.

```text
1978-1982
```

Use this for a year range.

```text
1984-06 to 1984-08
```

Use this for a month range.

## Use the best information you have

Do not let an uncertain date stop you from entering anything.

Examples:

```text
1976-08
```

A labeled vacation slide from August 1976.

```text
1989
```

A school portrait from the 1988–1989 school year.

```text
1940s
```

An old family photo from an approximate decade.

```text
1972-1975
```

A picture from sometime between two known family moves.

## Exact dates are not required

Many photo archives include pictures where the exact date is unknown.

That is normal.

A year, decade, or range can still be very helpful later.

## Dates and people’s ages

When People have birth information in Settings, dMPP can use the picture date to show age clues.

This can help you identify pictures more accurately.

For example, if a person appears to be about 8 years old and the age clue says they would have been 14, the picture date may need another look.

Age clues are only as good as the picture date and the person’s saved birth information.

## Dates from photo files

Some pictures contain date metadata from the camera or phone.

When available, dMPP may use that as a starting point.

For older scans, downloaded images, or copied files, the file date may not reflect when the picture was actually taken. In those cases, use the best historical date or era you know.

## Scanned pictures

Scanned pictures often have misleading file dates.

A scan date tells you when the image file was created, not when the original photo was taken.

For scanned photos, use clues such as:

- handwritten notes
- album context
- clothing
- holidays
- ages of people
- houses or locations
- known family events

## Invalid or unclear dates

If dMPP shows a date warning, check whether the date format is supported.

For example:

```text
1985-75
```

is not a valid month.

Use one of the supported date styles instead.

## Good habit

Start with the best date you know, even if it is approximate.

You can always refine the date later as you learn more.
